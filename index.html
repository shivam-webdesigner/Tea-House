The history of tea leaf in the world

The history of tea leaf dates back thousands of years. Originating in ancient China, tea was initially consumed for its medicinal properties. It later gained popularity as a recreational beverage during the Tang Dynasty.

Tea cultivation and trade expanded along the Silk Road, spreading its influence across Asia and eventually reaching Europe in the 17th century. The British East India Company played a significant role in promoting tea consumption worldwide. Today, tea remains one of the most widely consumed beverages globally, with diverse varieties and cultural traditions associated with its preparation and enjoyment.
